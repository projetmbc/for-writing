\begin{tdocnote}[Qualitative and semantic palettes]
    The threshold of \palMaxSemSize\ colors has been established for qualitative and semantic palettes. The following lines outline the rationale for this limit.
    %
    \begin{itemize}
        \item In qualitative color palettes, each color represents a single data point or a group of data. Experience shows that about ten colors are more than enough.

        \item Semantic palettes associate a color with a specific context, such as warning levels (see the frames in this document). Choosing \palMaxSemSize\ colors allows for \palSubSemSize\ different contexts, each with \palNbSemGrps\ sub-categories.
        Here are some examples of  sub-categories.
        %
        \begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{\small \arabic*0-\arabic*9}, widest=30-39, leftmargin=4.5em, nosep]

        \item \textbf{Initial\,/\,Idle:}
        default states such as ``To Do'', ``Clickable'', or ``To be analyzed''.

        \item \textbf{Active\,/\,Transition:}
        states in flux, including ``In Progress'' or UI ``Hover'' effects.

        \item \textbf{Success\,/\,Final:}
        terminal positive states like ``Done'', ``Clicked'', or ``Validated''.

        \item \textbf{Alert\,/\,Rejection:}
        error handling and negative outcomes, e.g., ``Error'', ``Inactive'', or ``Rejected''.
\end{enumerate}
    \end{itemize}
\end{tdocnote}
