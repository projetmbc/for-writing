\appendixsubsection{\palsunkept}

The following types of palettes are unkept.
%
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Exact duplicates:} identical color arrays.

    \item \textbf{Visual identical:} those indistinguishable to the naked eye.%
    \footnote{
        In these cases, the original palette is retained.
        For instance, as \matplotlib's \tdoccodein{text}{Cubehelix} is a specific implementation of the original \cubehelix's \tdoccodein{text}{Classic}, we keep the latter.
    }

    \item \textbf{Transformations:} reversals, shifts, or a combination of both.
\end{itemize}
%
We identify relations between palettes using the following notations.

\smallskip

\begin{tblr}{
  colspec = {*{4}{X[c]}},
  rows    = {m},
  cells   = {font = \small},
  row{2}  = {cmd = \tabsymb},
}
      Duplicates
    & Visually identical 
    & Reversal
%    & Shifted 
    & Reversal and shifted
    \\
      =
    & \cong
    & \rightleftharpoons
%    & \ll
    & \revshiftarrows
    \\
\end{tblr}
