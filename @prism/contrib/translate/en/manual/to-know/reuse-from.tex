\section{Reuse from...}

Here are the key points to remember when using palettes similar to those offered by projects listed in the section \ref{projects-used}.
%
\begin{enumerate}
	\item 
	\thisproj\ uses standardized \verb+CamelCase+ notation. Therefore, palette names such as \tdoccodein{py}{berlin} and \tdoccodein{py}{gist_heat} become \tdoccodein{text}{Berlin} and \tdoccodein{text}{GistHeat} respectively.

    
    \item \matplotlib\ palettes with a name ending with \verb+_r+ (reversed color order) are not included in \thisproj.

    
    \item The following presents palettes from projects other than \matplotlib\ that have been kept but renamed:
    \boxed{{\Rightarrow}\vphantom{pM}} indicates a name modification,
with the \thisproj\ name displayed on the right.
    %
    \subimport{../../../common/}{renamed-palettes.latex}

    
    \item The following palettes are excluded because they duplicate \thisproj\ palettes either directly or in reversed order,
    except that exact duplicates (same name and colors) are omitted when they don't come from \matplotlib, and we use
    \boxed{{=}\vphantom{pM}} for equality,
    \boxed{{\rightleftharpoons}\vphantom{pM}} for reversal,
    and the rightmost palettes are the ones retained in \thisproj.
    %
    \subimport{../../../common/}{ignored-palettes.latex}
\end{enumerate}
